Reflection (about 250 words)

When I was taking the photographs I wanted my subject to have all the attention, although I think that my subject should have been bigger. Even so, I feel that I took a photograph that allowed me to work with it. When taking photographs with shallow and deep depth of field I have to consider some factors, for example aperture and f/stops, which was a bit complicated for me when I was taking the photos. There are some steps you have to follow to get the result you expect, and many times I often forgot to adjust the ISO. Before learning the difference between these two DoF, I thought that natural light was enough, but it is not and I have to adjust the composition to add more light. Creating artificial DoF was a bit fun and I think it was the easiest part for me. I was able to learn how to give more focus and attention to my subject with a blurred background. In Camera Raw adjustments I add a bit of light, which led me to adjust the shadows, highlights and the contrast without losing details from the original photo. So I remembered that the histogram could help finish adjusting the image. I adjusted the shallow and highlights a bit more, trying to give the subject more brightness and emphasis, which was what I was looking for. I feel that I got a good result and it was close to how I had imagined it would look
